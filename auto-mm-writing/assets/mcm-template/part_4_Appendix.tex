\subsection*{What AI Was Used For}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Drafting and editing:} Refining narrative flow in the Introduction, Task descriptions, and Memo sections; improving grammar and academic tone.
    \item \textbf{Code scaffolding:} Generating initial templates for data preprocessing, loss function implementation, and attention mechanism modules; suggesting NumPy/PyTorch syntax.
    \item \textbf{LaTeX formatting:} Adjusting equation alignment, table layouts, and figure placement commands.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{What AI Was NOT Used For}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Data generation:} All numerical results come exclusively from the official \texttt{2026\_MCM\_Problem\_C\_Data.csv} and documented external sources (Wikidata, IMDb).
    \item \textbf{Model design decisions:} The choice of loss functions, regularization strategies, attention architecture, and hybrid policy were determined solely by the team.
    \item \textbf{Final conclusions:} All interpretations, recommendations, and the memo to producers reflect human judgment after reviewing model outputs.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Human Oversight and Verification}
Every AI-generated draft was reviewed, revised, and validated by team members. We cross-checked AI-suggested code against expected outputs and manually verified that all figures, tables, and numerical claims match our computations. The final paper represents our own analysis and reasoning, with AI serving only as an assistive tool.
